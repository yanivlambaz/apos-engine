# Purpose

This document defines the fundamental architecture of every APOS project.

The platform must be built around knowledge.

Pages are only one presentation layer.

Knowledge is the product.

---

# Traditional Website Architecture

Most websites organize information like this.

Home

↓

Services

↓

Blog

↓

Contact

↓

About

This architecture is navigation-driven.

It does not reflect how people think.

It does not reflect how AI understands information.

---

# APOS Architecture

Knowledge

↓

Topics

↓

Entities

↓

Relationships

↓

Content

↓

Pages

Pages are generated from knowledge.

Never the opposite.

---

# Principle 1

Knowledge exists independently from pages.

The same knowledge may appear inside:

Service Pages

FAQs

Articles

Guides

Landing Pages

Comparisons

Location Pages

AI Answers

Without becoming duplicate content.

---

# Principle 2

Every project has one Knowledge Graph.

Not multiple disconnected pages.

Every entity belongs somewhere inside the graph.

---

# Principle 3

Every page answers one dominant question.

Examples

How much does private nursing cost?

↓

One primary question

One complete answer

Supporting questions may exist.

The primary intent must remain obvious.

---

# Principle 4

Every page contributes to authority.

Pages are not isolated marketing assets.

They strengthen the knowledge graph.

# AI Readability

Knowledge must be easy to extract.

Avoid:

Large blocks of text.

Unclear structure.

Hidden relationships.

Use:

Short sections.

Meaningful headings.

Explicit entities.

Structured explanations.

Logical hierarchy.

---

# Authority Measurement

Authority is not measured by number of pages.

Authority is measured by:

Knowledge Coverage

Entity Coverage

Question Coverage

Relationship Density

Content Quality

Topical Completeness

---

# Knowledge Completeness

A topic is considered complete only when:

Primary question answered.

Secondary questions answered.

Related services linked.

Related FAQs linked.

Supporting guides exist.

Comparison exists.

Location relevance exists.

Conversion exists.

Trust elements exist.

---

# Anti-Patterns

The following patterns are prohibited.

Publishing articles without connecting them.

Creating service pages without FAQs.

Creating FAQs without services.

Creating city pages without local relevance.

Creating isolated landing pages.

Publishing duplicate explanations.

Every piece of knowledge must belong somewhere.

---

# Success Criteria

The knowledge graph should be understandable even if every page is removed.

Pages display knowledge.

Knowledge defines the platform.

This distinction is fundamental.

---

# Final Principle

Never ask:

"What page should we build?"

Always ask:

"What knowledge is still missing?"

Pages are the result.

Knowledge is the objective.
