# Purpose

A Service Page represents exactly one business service.

Its purpose is not to sell.

Its purpose is to eliminate uncertainty.

Visitors should feel informed enough to make a confident decision.

Sales are the consequence of clarity.

# Quick Facts Contract

Display immediately.

Maximum:

6 items.

Examples

Response Time

Availability

Service Area

Typical Duration

Suitable Age

Languages

Users should understand the service within thirty seconds.

---

# Service Summary Contract

Maximum

300 words.

Explain:

What

Who

Why

When

Outcome

No marketing language.

---

# Benefits Contract

Each benefit contains

Title

↓

Explanation

↓

Practical value

Never list features only.

Explain outcomes.

---

# Suitable For Contract

Examples

After surgery

Elderly care

Temporary assistance

Medication management

Every recommendation must explain why.

---

# Not Suitable For Contract

Transparency increases trust.

Whenever another service is more appropriate,

recommend it.

Never hide better alternatives.

---

# Process Contract

Every process uses numbered steps.

4

Maximum

7

Title

Description

# Pricing Contract

Preferred

Transparent pricing.

display:

# Definition of Done

A Service Page is complete only when:

The service is completely explained.

Users understand whether it is appropriate.

Users understand alternatives.

Users understand pricing expectations.

Users understand the process.

Users know the next step.

Search engines understand the service.

AI systems understand the service.

The page strengthens the authority of the platform.
