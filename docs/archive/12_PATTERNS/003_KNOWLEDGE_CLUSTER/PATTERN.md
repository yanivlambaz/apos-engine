# Purpose

A Knowledge Cluster is the primary authority-building unit within APOS.

The objective is not to create articles.

The objective is to completely own a topic.

A cluster should answer every meaningful question a visitor, search engine or AI system may have regarding one specific subject.

When a cluster is complete, users should not need to continue searching elsewhere.

---

# Definition

A Knowledge Cluster consists of one Pillar Guide and multiple supporting knowledge assets connected through meaningful semantic relationships.

The cluster behaves as one logical knowledge unit.

# Pillar Guide

Exactly one Pillar Guide exists for every cluster.

Purpose

Become the definitive resource.

Recommended length

3,000–8,000 words depending on complexity.

Must include

Overview

Definitions

Process

Benefits

Risks

Costs (where appropriate)

Alternatives

Common Mistakes

Expert Advice

FAQ

Resources

Related Services

Next Steps

---

# Supporting Articles

Each article answers exactly one question.

Examples

"What is..."

"When should..."

"How much..."

"Can I..."

"Which..."

"What happens if..."

One article.

One intent.

No mixed intent.

---

# FAQ Collection

Minimum

20 questions

Recommended

40+

Questions grouped into logical categories.

Each answer should be concise but complete.

FAQ supports the Pillar Guide.

Never replaces it.

---

# Comparison Pages

Purpose

Support decision making.

Examples

Private Nurse vs Caregiver

Tax Refund vs Tax Credit

Home Care vs Hospital Care

Comparison pages must remain objective.

---

# Glossary

Every important industry term receives its own definition.

Definitions should be:

Short

Clear

Accurate

Linked to related content

AI-readable

---

# Checklist

Every cluster should contain practical downloadable or printable checklists whenever appropriate.

Examples

Preparing for Home Care

Required Tax Documents

Hospital Discharge Checklist

Checklist content increases usefulness.

---

# Related Services

Every cluster must connect to relevant services.

Educational content supports commercial content.

Commercial content never dominates educational content.

---

# Internal Linking Rules

Every article links to:

Pillar Guide

Related Articles

Relevant FAQ

Relevant Services

Glossary

Comparison Pages

Every internal link must improve understanding.

Never create links solely for SEO.

---

# Entity Coverage

Every cluster must define:

Primary Entity

Secondary Entities

Supporting Entities

Related Organizations

Relevant Locations

Relevant Processes

Relevant Documents

Authority grows through entity completeness.

---

# Search Intent Coverage

Each cluster should satisfy:

Informational Intent

Commercial Investigation

Navigational Intent (where applicable)

Transactional Intent (through Service Pages)

No important intent should remain unanswered.

---

# Content Freshness

Every cluster defines:

Created Date

Last Reviewed

Review Frequency

Responsible Editor

Important clusters should be reviewed regularly.

---

# AI Optimization

Every cluster should answer:

What is this topic?

Why is it important?

Who needs it?

When is it relevant?

How does it work?

What are the risks?

What are the alternatives?

What should users do next?

Answers must be explicit.

# Definition of Done

A Knowledge Cluster is complete only when:

Every major user question has been answered.

Every important entity has been defined.

Every supporting resource is connected.

The Pillar Guide has become the central authority on the topic.

Users can confidently make informed decisions without leaving the platform.

The cluster strengthens both human understanding and machine understanding.
